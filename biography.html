Biography

James Mayhew is a promising young pianist currently in his first year at the prestigious Royal Academy of Music, where he studies under the tutelage of the renowned pianist Ian Fountain. His musical journey has been marked by dedication, exceptional talent, and a deep passion for both performance and composition.

For four formative years, James honed his craft at Wells Cathedral School under the expert guidance of Hilary Coates and Joseph Tong. His time there culminated in reaching the finals of the school's highly competitive concerto competition, where he delivered a stunning performance of the first movement of Tchaikovsky's Piano Concerto No. 1 – one of the most demanding and beloved works in the piano repertoire.

James's competitive spirit and musical excellence were further demonstrated when he secured second place at the Oxford Piano Competition for Aspiring Professionals, competing against talented pianists from across the country. This achievement underscores his technical proficiency and interpretive maturity as a performer.

Beyond his role as a performer, James has established himself as a composer of considerable promise. He has showcased his original compositions in numerous performances, with his Fugue-Fantasy standing as a particularly notable work that demonstrates his understanding of both traditional forms and contemporary musical language.

What sets James apart is his adventurous approach to repertoire. He actively seeks out and explores lesser-known works, particularly those by composers such as Charles-Valentin Alkan and Samuil Feinberg – composers renowned for creating music of extraordinary technical and interpretive difficulty. This dedication to expanding the boundaries of standard repertoire speaks to his commitment to musical exploration and his fearless approach to challenging material.

Collaboration lies at the heart of James's musical philosophy. He actively seeks opportunities to work with other musicians, understanding that the exchange of ideas and the sharing of musical experiences enriches both his own artistry and that of his collaborators. This collaborative spirit has led to numerous chamber music performances and continues to be a driving force in his musical development.

As James continues his studies at the Royal Academy of Music, audiences can look forward to witnessing the continued evolution of an artist who combines technical excellence with genuine musical curiosity, promising performances that are both technically accomplished and deeply moving.
